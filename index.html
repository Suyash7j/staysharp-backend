Vocal
Nursery Rhyme Stretch
▾
What it is Recite a nursery rhyme while deliberately stretching each vowel sound — slow it down until each word feels overlong. How to do it
  1. Pick any nursery rhyme (e.g. "Humpty Dumpty")
  2. Say it at normal speed once through
  3. Repeat, stretching every vowel 2–3x longer than normal
  4. Focus on your mouth shape and breath control
Why it works Loosens jaw tension, improves articulation, and warms up your vocal range before important conversations.
Vocal
Stressing the Verb
▾
What it is Say the same sentence multiple times, stressing a different word (especially verbs) each time to change meaning and impact. How to do it
  1. Pick a sentence: "I never said she took the money"
  2. Say it 6 times, emphasizing a different word each time
  3. Notice how meaning shifts with each stress
  4. Apply to a sentence from work or a presentation
Why it works Trains you to speak with intentionality — commanding presence comes from knowing which word to land on.
Non-Vocal
The "Box" Gesture
▾
What it is A structured hand gesture that frames your ideas visually, used by top communicators to signal confidence and control. How to do it
  1. Hold both hands in front of you, chest height
  2. Form a rectangular "box" shape with your hands
  3. When making a point, move hands to outline the box
  4. Practice while talking — it should feel natural, not forced
Why it works Controlled gestures signal authority. The box keeps your hands in the "power zone" and prevents nervous fidgeting.
Non-Vocal
3-Second Eye Lock
▾
What it is Holding eye contact for exactly 3 seconds before naturally breaking — enough to feel confident without being intense. How to do it
  1. In any conversation, pick one person to focus on
  2. Make eye contact and silently count: 1, 2, 3
  3. Break naturally (look to the side, not down)
  4. Repeat — aim for 3 locks per conversation today
Why it works Eye contact is the fastest signal of confidence. Breaking down signals submission; breaking to the side signals certainty.
